# Switchback Cycle — Profitability Analysis

A data visualization project analyzing Switchback Cycle's profitability through three strategic levers: Volume, Price, and Cost. Built in Tableau for the company's executive leadership team.

**Section C, Team 2:** Xinghan Shen, Alexander Lim, Jessica Xiong, Neha Maqsood, John Saleeb

---

## Project Overview

Switchback Cycle operates a wholesale distribution model across four product lines and six geographic regions. This analysis diagnoses the primary constraint on profitability, quantifies the financial impact of each strategic lever, and delivers a prioritized recommendation to leadership.

**Business baseline:**

| Total Revenue | Total COGS | Gross Profit | Gross Margin |
|--------------|------------|--------------|--------------|
| $81.0M | $48.1M | $32.1M | 40.5% |

---

## Key Findings

Revenue is heavily concentrated in Road (42%) and Mountain (40%) bikes, with the US accounting for 66.6% of total revenue ($53.9M). The reseller base is well-diversified with no single partner exceeding $882K annually.

**Scenario modeling against the $32.1M profit baseline:**

| Lever | Projected Profit | Incremental Gain |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Volume +5% | $33,743,196 | +$1,606,819 |
| Price +2% | $33,742,003 | +$1,605,626 |
| Cost -3% | $33,580,724 | +$1,444,347 |

While volume and price produce marginally larger gains, **cost reduction was identified as the primary lever** because it addresses the structural root cause — high unit costs in the Touring and Road lines, the two largest revenue contributors. Additionally, the 5% of orders receiving discounts see margin drop from 41.5% to 28.3%, forfeiting $527,508 in revenue annually.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Immediate — Supply Chain & Freight:** Renegotiate vendor contracts and freight terms for Touring and Road lines, targeting a 3% unit cost reduction to generate $1.44M in incremental profit.
2. **Short-term — Margin Floors:** Pause discounts on products below target gross margin until unit costs are stabilized, recovering the $527K currently lost to undisciplined discounting.
3. **Medium-term — Volume Scaling:** Once cost structure is optimized, grow volume in highest-margin lines, particularly Accessories/Components at 58.7% margin.
